About The Project

The Science Behind Driver Safety

DrowseGuard uses real-time computer vision and machine learning to detect signs of drowsiness before accidents happen. Here's how it works under the hood.

Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR)

The mathematical foundation of drowsiness detection

EAR = (||P2-P6|| + ||P3-P5||) / (2 × ||P1-P4||)
📐

The Formula

EAR measures the ratio of eye opening to eye width using 6 specific facial landmarks around each eye.

🎯

The Threshold

An EAR below 0.22 indicates closed/nearly-closed eyes. If sustained for 20+ frames, drowsiness is declared.

⚡

Real-time Speed

Processing runs at up to 30 FPS, providing near-instantaneous response to eye closure events.
